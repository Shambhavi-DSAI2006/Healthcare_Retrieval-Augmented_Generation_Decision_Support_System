3NF Notes — Healthcare RAG System Database (Updated to Final ER Diagram)

All tables in the schema are designed to satisfy Third Normal Form (3NF). 
Each table ensures:

1. No repeating groups (1NF)
2. No partial dependencies (2NF)
3. No transitive dependencies (3NF)

---

1. Source(source_id, name, type, url, source_reliability)
   - PK: source_id
   - Functional Dependencies:
       source_id → name, type, url, source_reliability
   - ✅ 3NF

---

2. Document(document_id, title, publication_date, source_id, document_type, medical_speciality)
   - PK: document_id
   - FDs:
       document_id → title, publication_date, source_id, document_type, medical_speciality
   - ✅ 3NF

---

3. Section(section_id, section_title, document_id)
   - PK: section_id
   - FDs:
       section_id → section_title, document_id
   - ✅ 3NF

---

4. Chunk(chunk_id, chunk_text, chunk_index, section_id)
   - PK: chunk_id
   - FDs:
       chunk_id → chunk_text, chunk_index, section_id
   - ✅ 3NF

---

5. Embedding(embedding_id, chunk_id, vector)
   - PK: embedding_id
   - FDs:
       embedding_id → chunk_id, vector
       chunk_id → embedding_id, vector   (due to 1:1 relationship)
   - ✅ 3NF

---

6. Topic(topic_id, topic_name)
   - PK: topic_id
   - FDs:
       topic_id → topic_name
   - ✅ 3NF

---

7. Chunk_Topic(chunk_id, topic_id)
   - Composite PK: (chunk_id, topic_id)
   - FDs:
       (chunk_id, topic_id) → no non-key attributes
   - Represents M:N relationship between Chunk and Topic
   - ✅ 3NF

---

8. Query(query_id, query_text, timestamp)
   - PK: query_id
   - FDs:
       query_id → query_text, timestamp
   - ✅ 3NF

---

9. Response(response_id, query_id, generated_answer, confidence_score)
   - PK: response_id
   - FDs:
       response_id → query_id, generated_answer, confidence_score
       query_id → response_id, generated_answer, confidence_score   (1:1 relationship)
   - ✅ 3NF

---

10. Retrieved_Chunk(query_id, chunk_id, relevance_score)
    - Composite PK: (query_id, chunk_id)
    - FDs:
        (query_id, chunk_id) → relevance_score
    - Represents M:N retrieval relationship between Query and Chunk
    - ✅ 3NF

---

11. Evaluation(evaluation_id, response_id, factuality_score, consistency_score, notes, response_timestamp)
    - PK: evaluation_id
    - FDs:
        evaluation_id → response_id, factuality_score, consistency_score, notes, response_timestamp
        response_id → evaluation_id, factuality_score, consistency_score, notes, response_timestamp (1:1 relationship)
    - ✅ 3NF

---

Conclusion:

- All tables satisfy 3NF requirements:
  ✔ Every non-key attribute depends only on the primary key  
  ✔ No partial dependencies exist  
  ✔ No transitive dependencies exist  

- M:N relationships are correctly decomposed using junction tables:
  - Chunk_Topic
  - Retrieved_Chunk

- 1:1 relationships are explicitly captured using functional dependencies:
  - Chunk ↔ Embedding
  - Query ↔ Response
  - Response ↔ Evaluation

Thus, the schema is fully normalized up to Third Normal Form (3NF) and ensures minimal redundancy and maximum data integrity.
